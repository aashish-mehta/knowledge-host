The Avogadro constant ($N_A$ or $L$) is the number of constituent particles, usually atoms or molecules, that are contained in the amount of substance given by one [[Mole]]. It is exactly $6.02214076 \times 10^{23}$ per mole.

Essentially, the number of elements required to reach its [[Average Atomic Mass|Atomic Weight]] in grams (e.g. $N_A$ Lithium atoms will have a mass of ~6.94g).

## Value

$N_A = 6.02214076 \times 10^{23} \, \text{mol}^{-1}$

## Symbol(s)

$N_A$, $L$
