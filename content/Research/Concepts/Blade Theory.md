A [[Pump]] mechanism usually contains an [[impeller]] and a [[diffuser]], each consisting of a hub, blades and a shroud.

![[impeller-geometry.png | center | desc | 512]]An example geometry of an impeller/rotor ([[References#19]])

The blade geometry can be described using a meridional surface, which describes the inner and outer radii, and the blade length. 

The blade is also characterised by the [[flow angle]]. This is true for both the impeller and diffuser geometries. Changing any of these characteristic lengths or angles will impact the [[flow rate]] and resultant [[pressure head]] across the device.
