A [[Design of Experiments|DoE]] approach in which designs are generated based on an optimality criterion. A sparse method often used when there are resource limitations.

In D-Optimal, the determinant of the matrix of parameters is maximised.

## Advantages
- Useful when there are resource limitations
- Can be tailored to optimise the parameters of interest

## Disadvantages
- Will not represent the design space as thoroughly as other approaches, such as [[Central Composite]]
- Not very structured, can vary depending on the optimisation method
